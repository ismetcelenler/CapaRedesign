Exploring the Universe's Greatest Mysteries

Center for Astroparticles and High Energy Physics

CAPA is a leading research institute at the University of Zaragoza, dedicated to advancing our understanding of fundamental physics through cutting-edge research in astroparticles, dark matter, and high-energy phenomena.

Research Areas

Our interdisciplinary research spans multiple frontiers of modern physics, from the smallest particles to the largest structures in the universe.

Dark Matter Research

Investigating the mysterious dark matter that comprises 85% of all matter in the universe through innovative detection methods and theoretical frameworks.

Cosmic Ray Physics

Studying ultra-high-energy cosmic rays and their origins, using advanced detection arrays and computational modeling to understand these cosmic messengers.

Quantum Technologies

Developing cutting-edge quantum technologies for fundamental physics research, including quantum sensors and detection systems.

Astroparticle Physics

Exploring the intersection of particle physics and astrophysics to understand fundamental processes in extreme cosmic environments.

Theoretical Physics

Advancing theoretical frameworks in particle physics, cosmology, and quantum field theory to interpret experimental observations.

Experimental Techniques

Developing innovative experimental methods and detector technologies for next-generation physics experiments.
